## 1. Overview

This Addendum applies when you open a share certificate that is eligible for a
**Certificate of Deposit Token (“CDT”)**. The CDT is a digital record of your
certificate. **Your deposit remains at [Issuing CU]** and is federally insured
by the NCUA up to applicable limits **as a share certificate on our books**.
**The CDT token itself is not insured.**

## 2. What the CDT is

- A digital asset associated with your certificate’s deposit identifier.  
- Evidence of terms (principal, rate, term, early-withdrawal penalty) as
  recorded when issued.  
- Held in a wallet you control (or a wallet service you choose).  

Losing wallet access may require our recovery procedures; it does not by itself
cancel your insured deposit on our books if we can re-verify you.

## 3. Network presentment (cash-out at other credit unions)

If we participate in the **CDT Settlement Network**, you may present your CDT
at a **Participating Redeeming Credit Union** to request cash-out.

By holding a network-enabled CDT, you authorize:

1. The redeeming credit union to request claim verification from us;  
2. Us to disclose limited information needed to verify the certificate and
   quote cash-out (not your full CIP file beyond need-to-know);  
3. Cash-out under **mature** or **early withdrawal** terms in your certificate
   disclosures (early withdrawal may reduce interest by the disclosed penalty);  
4. **Burn** of the CDT (destruction of the digital receipt) as a condition of
   final settlement, so the certificate cannot be cashed twice;  
5. Settlement between us and the redeeming credit union for the cash-out
   amount.

## 4. Free transfer of the token

The CDT may be technologically transferable between wallets. **Transfer of the
token does not automatically transfer membership, insurance coverage, or the
deposit liability on our books.** We may refuse presentment or recovery to
anyone we cannot identify as you (or your legal representative) under our CIP
and security procedures.

Payment terminals or other parties may check attestation status before
accepting a CDT; that check does not move your deposit.

## 5. Presentment is not a bank wire of “crypto principal”

When a redeeming credit union credits your account or pays cash, that
institution is **advancing or paying under network rules**. Your **insured
relationship for the certificate remains with us** until we pay and close the
certificate on our core system.

## 6. Your responsibilities

- Keep wallet credentials secure.  
- Complete identity verification at any redeeming institution.  
- Co-sign burn/redeem instructions when requested (or use official recovery).  
- Understand early-withdrawal costs before presentment prior to maturity.  

## 7. Our responsibilities

- Maintain accurate certificate records.  
- Honor valid network presentments per network agreements and law.  
- Apply the same rate and penalty math disclosed at account opening.  
- Not market the token as NCUA-insured crypto.

## 8. Limits and refusal

We or a redeeming institution may refuse or delay presentment if: OFAC or
fraud concerns arise; the certificate is already closed; burn cannot be
completed; network outage; or legal process requires freeze.

## 9. E-SIGN

You consent to electronic disclosures and signatures for CDT and presentment
documents, consistent with our E-SIGN consent (if separately obtained).

## 10. Hierarchy

If this Addendum conflicts with the Network’s rules, **your certificate
agreement and applicable law control your deposit relationship with us**.
Network fees charged between institutions are not additional early-withdrawal
penalties unless disclosed as such.

## 11. Acknowledgment

By signing or clicking accept, you acknowledge you have read this Addendum and
the insurance disclosure: **deposit insured (limits apply); token not insured.**
